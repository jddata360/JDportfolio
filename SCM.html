Results

Grocery Data Analysis : This output provides a comprehensive analysis of a grocery data analysis, The final outcome of analysis is the ABC-FSN Matrix Heatmap, which provides a combined classification of grocery products based on both their Monetary Value (ABC) and their Movement Rate (FSN)..

The heatmap shows the count of products that fall into each of the nine categories, with darker shades indicating a higher number of products in that group.

Interpreting the ABC-FSN Matrix Heatmap

Axis Classification Criteria
Y-Axis (Rows) ABC (A, B, C) Based on Revenue (final_amount). A: High Value, B: Medium Value, C: Low Value
X-Axis (Columns) FSN (F, S, N) Based on Quantity Sold (quantity). F: Fast Moving, S: Slow Moving, N: Non-Moving.

Interpretation Summary Table The table below translates the product counts in the matrix into key strategic actions:

Inventory Strategy by Category

Cell Classification Product Count Strategic Implication
AF A-Fast (High Value, Fast Moving) 9 CRITICAL FOCUS: These are the money-makers and top sellers. Implement strict control, accurate forecasting, JIT (Just-In-Time) inventory, and minimum safety stock to maximize cash flow and avoid stockouts
AS A-Slow (High Value, Slow Moving) 4 MONITOR CAREFULLY: High monetary investment but slower sales. Requires management-level attention. Check for obsolescence or potential overstocking.
AN A-Non-Moving (High Value, Non-Moving) 0 MAJOR ALARM: No products here is ideal. If a product fell here, it would mean high capital is tied up in items that aren't selling. Immediate action (promotion/liquidation) required.
BF B-Fast (Medium Value, Fast Moving) 2 ROUTINE CONTROL: Use standard reorder points (ROP) and economic order quantity (EOQ). Automate processes, but review periodically.
BS B-Slow (Medium Value, Slow Moving) 0 WATCH CLOSELY: Needs periodic review. Evaluate if they should be promoted or gradually downgraded to 'C' control.
BN B-Non-Moving (Medium Value, Non-Moving) 1 PROMOTION CANDIDATE: Moderate value tied up in stagnant stock. Look for opportunities to clear stock with minor discounts.
CF C-Fast (Low Value, Fast Moving) 0** SIMPLIFIED CONTROL: These are low-cost, high-volume items (e.g., staples). Use simple visual control or two-bin systems.
CS C-Slow (Low Value, Slow Moving) 0 LOOSE CONTROL: Manage with minimal effort. Could be seasonal stock.
CN C-Non-Moving (Low Value, Non-Moving) 2 PHASE OUT/DISPOSE: Least critical items. Consider immediate clearance, disposal, or discontinuation to free up warehouse space and reduce administrative effort.

  • Final Decision
  • Concentration on 'AF' (9 products)
  • Inventory management must primarily focus on the 9 High Value, Fast Moving products (e.g., Tomatoes, Bread, Chicken Breast).
  • Errors here (stockouts) lead to the greatest loss of potential revenue.
  • Focus on 'CN' (2 products) : - Having 2 Low Value, Non-Moving products (e.g., Ground Beef, Carrots) that are tying up space without contributing much revenue. These are candidates for elimination or deep markdown.
  • The ABC-FSN matrix moves you from general inventory policies to item-specific, targeted strategies, leading to lower inventory costs, fewer stockouts, and improved working capital.
